Why your original FAB was invisible (likely causes) â€” and why this fix works
Parent positioning matters
In React Native an absolutely positioned child is positioned relative to the nearest ancestor with a position value other than the default. If your SafeAreaView or root container did not have position: 'relative', the FAB's position: 'absolute' might not be placed where you expected or could be clipped/out of view. Setting container: { position: 'relative', flex: 1 } ensures the FAB is positioned relative to the full screen area.
Don't put the FAB inside the ScrollView or content that scrolls
If the FAB is inside the ScrollView it will move/scroll with content or be covered by other layers. Placing the FAB as a sibling (outside) of the ScrollView and after it in the JSX ensures it's drawn on top and stays fixed while the ScrollView scrolls.
Android stacking (elevation) vs iOS stacking (zIndex)
On Android you need elevation for visual stacking and shadows; on iOS you need zIndex and shadow* properties. Using both (elevation + zIndex) gives cross-platform coverage so the button is visually on top on both platforms.
Render order matters
Siblings rendered later in JSX are drawn above earlier siblings. Putting the FAB after the main content ensures it is visually above. (Modal components are separate and already rendered above the UI â€” your chat modal uses Modal so it will overlay everything when opened.)
Potential clipping / overflow issues
If any ancestor had overflow: 'hidden' or used transforms, it could clip/alter the stacking context. Keeping the FAB at the root SafeAreaView level avoids those pitfalls.
Padding bottom so content isn't hidden
Add paddingBottom to your ScrollView content to prevent the bottom-most content from being visually obscured by the FAB.
Extra tips / common gotchas
If you have other views with zIndex or transforms, re-check them â€” transformed views can create new stacking contexts that affect zIndex behavior. Keep the FAB as high in the tree as reasonable (e.g., direct child of SafeAreaView) to avoid surprises.
On Android, elevation controls both shadow and draw order; if you still see the FAB beneath something, raise the elevation number on the FAB or lower it on the overlapping sibling.
If you prefer a true "portal" behavior (render at the very top of the app no matter where you call it from), consider libraries like react-native-portalize or React Native Paper's Portal â€” but your constraints said no external libs, so the above approach is correct.
